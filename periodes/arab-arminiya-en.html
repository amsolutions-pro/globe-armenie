Arab Arminiya

640 – 884
Umayyad then Abbasid caliphate (ostikanate)

The first Arab raids reached Dvin as early as 640. Around 700 the caliphate organised the province of Arminiya — Armenia, Georgian Iberia and Caucasian Albania — governed by an ostikan from Dvin, then Bardaa. In 705, to break the nobility, the Arabs burned hundreds of nakharars alive in the churches of Nakhichevan and Khram.

The nakharar revolts (703, 748, 774–775 — crushed at Bagrevand) bled the old Mamikonian and Kamsarakan houses… to the profit of the more prudent Bagratids: Ashot Msaker received the caliphate's title of "prince of Armenia" (ishkhan) around 804-806. The Bagratid path was not straightforward, though: between 852 and 855, Abbasid general Bugha al-Kabir led a fresh crackdown, deporting several Armenian princes, including Bagratids, and the catholicos himself, to Samarra. The dynasty recovered: its descendants secured the higher title of "prince of princes" before reclaiming the crown in 884.
