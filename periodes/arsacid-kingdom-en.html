Arsacid Kingdom

AD 66 – 428
Arsacid dynasty, between Rome and Persia

In AD 66 Tiridates I, brother of the Parthian king, received the crown of Armenia from Nero in Rome: a founding compromise — Parthian dynasty, Roman investiture.

In 301, Tiridates III, converted by St Gregory the Illuminator, made Christianity the state religion: a world first. In 405 the monk Mesrop Mashtots created the Armenian alphabet, still in use. In 387, Rome and Sasanian Persia partitioned the kingdom — about a fifth in the west for Rome, the rest under Persian sway. The monarchy was abolished by Persia in 428, at the nakharars' own request.
