Ottoman–Persian partitions

1514 – 1828
Ottoman Empire (west) · Safavid then Qajar Persia (east)

After the battle of Chaldiran (1514) Ottomans and Safavids fought over the plateau; the peace of Zuhab (1639) froze a lasting partition: Ottoman Western Armenia, Persian Eastern Armenia. In 1604 Shah Abbas deported hundreds of thousands of Armenians to Isfahan — New Julfa, which would dominate the silk trade as far as India.

In 1828 the treaty of Turkmenchay ceded the khanates of Erivan and Nakhichevan to Russia: the territorial matrix of modern Armenia. From it would come the First Republic (1918) and independent Armenia (1991).
