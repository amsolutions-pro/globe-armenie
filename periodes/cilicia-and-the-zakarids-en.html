Cilicia and the Zakarids

1080 – 1375
Armenian Kingdom of Cilicia · Zakarid principality · Mongols

Fleeing the Seljuk invasions, Armenian princes settled in Cilicia on the Mediterranean: the Rubenid principality (1080) became a kingdom when Leo I the Magnificent was crowned in 1198. Allied to the Crusaders and then to the Mongols, the state prospered through its ports — Ayas, described by Marco Polo.

On the highlands, the Zakarian brothers, generals of Queen Tamar of Georgia, liberated the north (1199–1236) before the Mongol wave. Sis fell to the Mamluks in 1375; the last king, Leo V of Lusignan, died in exile in Paris in 1393 — his tomb is at Saint-Denis. With the fall of Sis the last independent Armenian state died out: none would rise again until 1918.
