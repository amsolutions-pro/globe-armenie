Artaxiad Kingdom

189 – 1 BC
Artaxiad dynasty · apogee under Tigranes II the Great

In 189 BC, after Antiochos III's defeat by Rome, Artaxias I proclaimed himself king and, according to Strabo, "gathered the lands where one and the same language was spoken, Armenian".

Apogee under Tigranes II the Great (95–55 BC): allied to Mithridates of Pontus, he conquered Syria, Cilicia and Atropatene and proclaimed himself "king of kings" and founded around 83 BC his imperial capital, Tigranakert, peopled with deported Greeks. His empire stretched from the Caspian to the Mediterranean — before Lucullus (69) and Pompey (66 BC) reduced it to Greater Armenia.
