Satrapy of Armenia

590 – 331 BC
Median then Achaemenid empire · Orontid dynasty

After Urartu's fall the plateau passed to the Medes, then to Achaemenid Persia. The trilingual Behistun inscription (520 BC) mentions the name "Armina" for the first time.

The local Orontid (Yervanduni) dynasty, traditionally linked to a Persian dignitary named Orontas mentioned in Xenophon's Anabasis (with no firmly established connection to the satrap of Armenia), governed the satrapy, which paid 400 talents of silver according to Herodotus (Histories, III, 93) and reportedly supplied colts each year for the feasts of Mithra, according to a figure from Strabo (Geography, XI, 14, 9) whose precise number is disputed by modern philology. Xenophon and the Ten Thousand crossed its snowy villages in the winter of 401-400 BC.
