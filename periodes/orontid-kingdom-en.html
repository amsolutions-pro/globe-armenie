Orontid Kingdom

331 – 200 BC
Orontid (Yervanduni) dynasty, de facto independence

Armenia remained nominally under Macedonian administration after Gaugamela (331 BC), but it was Alexander's death (323 BC) and the chaos of the following Diadochi wars that let satrap Orontes (Yervand) become independent in fact: Armenia was never really conquered by the Macedonians.

The Orontids reigned as kings between the Seleucids to the south and Pontus to the north, in measured Hellenisation — the rocks of Armavir bear Greek inscriptions quoting Euripides and Hesiod. Around 200 BC Antiochos III deposed the last Orontid and entrusted the country to his generals Artaxias and Zariadres — who would soon betray him.
