Kingdom of Urartu

c. 860 – 590 BC
Independent kingdom (Biainili)

The kingdom of Urartu (Biainili in Urartian, the biblical "kingdom of Ararat") united the highlands around Lake Van under Sarduri I around 860 BC. Assyria's great rival, it excelled in hydraulics — the Menua canal, named for a king who reigned a few decades later, over 70 km long, still irrigates the Van region —, in bronze metallurgy and in fortress architecture. King Argishti I founded the fortress of Erebuni there in 782 BC — the birth of Yerevan, 29 years before Rome's legendary founding date (753 BC) — under the protection of the national god Haldi.

Weakened by Cimmerian and Scythian raids, the kingdom collapsed around 590 BC, probably under Median pressure, in circumstances still debated for lack of cuneiform sources covering this final period. It is on this substrate that the Armenian people emerges into the sources.
