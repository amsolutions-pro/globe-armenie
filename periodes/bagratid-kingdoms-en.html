Bagratid Kingdoms

884 – 1045
Bagratid dynasty and sister kingdoms

In 884 Ashot I Bagratuni was crowned "king of the Armenians", recognised by both caliph and Byzantine emperor. Ani became the capital in 961: the "city of 1001 churches", a Silk Road stage of perhaps 100,000 inhabitants.

The kingdom split into sister realms — Vaspurakan (908), Kars (963), Lori (978), Syunik (987) — easy prey: Byzantium annexed Vaspurakan (1021) then Ani (1045); the Seljuks sacked Ani in 1064 and crushed Byzantium at Manzikert (1071), opening Anatolia to the Turks.
